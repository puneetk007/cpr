WHAT IS LEPTIN?

Leptin is our “starvation hormone”. It is essentially, a protein that’s released by the fat cells into our bloodstream. When it reaches the brain it signals that the body has enough energy stored in the fat cells to engage in normal day to day activities.

On reaching certain leptin threshold, the brain senses that your body has reached energy sufficiency to burn energy at a normal rate, eat food at a normal amount and can also engage in exercise at a normal or expensive rate.

But people tend to eat less when they are on a diet. The fat cells then lose some fat and release less leptin. This reduces your personal leptin threshold, which your brain senses and puts your body in a starvation state which initiates several processes in your body to drive leptin levels back up.

One such process is stimulating the vagus nerve, which runs between your brain and the abdomen. It is your energy storage nerve and when it turned on, it makes you hungrier. It is designed to take up extra energy (the food you eat) and store it in fat cells (making you fat). To re-establish the leptin threshold to where it belongs, it causes you to eat more.

WHAT IS LEPTIN RESISTANCE?

People produce leptin in proportion to their sizes and since obese people’s fat cells store higher amounts of fat, the amount of leptin they produce is also relatively higher.

Given how leptin is supposed to work, the obese population should naturally limit the amount of food they consume. In other words, their brains should already know that they have enough energy stored.

But it might be possible, their leptin signaling has stopped working although there is plenty of leptin present, the brain might not register it. This condition is termed as Leptin resistance and is considered to be one of the main biological contributors to obesity.

This makes the brain to think erroneously that your body is starving even though more than enough energy is stored. This causes the brain to change its behavior and triggers the following responses in order to regain body fat.

  • Eating more: In order to prevent starvation, your brain thinks that you should eat more.
  • Reduced energy expenditure: your brain starts burning less energy in your resting period in order to conserve more energy.

Thus leptin resistance which is a hormonal defect is the underlying cause of the weight gain.
